We are hiring postdoctoral fellows and full-stack web developers to develop interactive visualization methods and tools for multi-omics datasets generated in cancer immunotherapy clinical trials. This NIH-funded project is highly interdisciplinary and being conducted in close collaboration with computational and experimental domain experts, providing an excellent opportunity for collaborative research. A key collaborator is Sacha Gnjatic, the Associate Director of the Human Immune Monitoring Center at Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai. If interested, please send a CV to zeynep.gumus AT mssm.edu.

Roles & Responsibilities:
Both full-stack web developers and data visualization postdoctoral fellows will be responsible for the development, implementation and support of new technologies and web-based applications to facilitate user-intuitive exploration of multi-omics datasets in cancer immunotherapy clinical trials.

  • Create data visualization approaches and web-based tools from design research through ideating and prototyping, iterating/debugging and implementation.
  • Integration of such tools with data analysis and management tools.
  • Provide daily support in the maintenance of the tools developed; identify system deficiencies, design and code technical solutions and/or implement other effective solutions, writing manuscripts.
  • Train users in the developed web tools. Utilize user feedback to enhance tool effectiveness.
  • Ensure data confidentiality and security of data processed, stored or accessed by the network.
  • Perform other related duties.

Requirements:
  • Bachelor’s Degree in Computer Science or related technical discipline. M.S. or Ph.D. preferred.
  • 2 years of experience in the development of web-based applications and/or data visualization.
  • Strong working knowledge of Javascript and its libraries.
  • Excellent written and verbal communication skills
  • Understanding of common software development practices
  • Strong conceptualization ability, strong visual communication ability
  • Exceptional design skills, production value, and attention to detail
  • Experience with user interface design patterns and standard user-centered design methodologies
  • Knowledge/experience with multi-omics datasets (proteomics, genomics, etc) is not necessary but desired.
